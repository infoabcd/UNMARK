The source is a photograph or scan of genuine pencil, pen, manga, or line artwork. Remove only the later-added digital marker strokes that are visibly thicker, smoother, solid black, opaque, and more uniform in width than the original artwork. Remove all connected parts of those thick overlay strokes, including long diagonal lines, loops, zigzags, circles, and crossed-out marks.

Preserve every original fine, textured, pressure-varying line exactly, especially the subject's face, hair, neck, shoulders, arms, hands, clothing, contours, shading, signature, paper texture, and any real pen or marker visible in the photograph. Do not interpret an arm, body contour, hair strand, clothing fold, or original dark shading as an unwanted mark. Do not redraw, simplify, crop, zoom, move, or change the subject. Reconstruct only the narrow regions covered by the thick digital marker strokes. If a stroke cannot be separated from the original line art with confidence, leave that area unchanged.
